The dialog of this movie is fucking shit.

The execution of the story line isn't much better either, there was potential here but for me it just wasn't immersive or satisfying. Beyond this, the world building left a lot to be desired reducing the immersion even further. For example, why was the bar always full of patrons if it was such a violent unsafe environment? Not to mention that the musicians of this island must be the bravest musicians ever. Every night there were bands playing but almost every night the band would be verbally and/or physically abused... yet they kept going. I understand that we have to suspend our disbelief to enjoy fiction but leaving these questions unanswered felt sloppy.

Overall, the fight scenes were the most entertaining scenes of the movie and were well choreographed and great action. I suppose this is the whole point of a movie like this, a movie to sit back, turn your brain off and enjoy the action. So in that respect, this movie is a success.

My favorite character in this movie would be Moe, played by Arturo Castro (XII). Moe served as the comedy relief character to break the tension and get some laughs, albeit cheap laughs. I found him likeable and relatable. However, there were times the character very much felt completely out of place.

I have mixed feelings about this movie. Despite all the short comings, I did enjoy watching it but I can't bring myself to give it more than 2.5 stars unfortunately.
