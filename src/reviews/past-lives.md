*Past Lives* is Celine Song's debut feature, and it announces an astonishing new voice in cinema. This is a film of almost radical restraint — barely any plot, no villain, no big dramatic crisis — and yet it left me completely undone.

## The Story

Nora and Hae Sung were childhood sweethearts in Seoul. Life separates them, brings them briefly back together through video calls, then pulls them apart again. Years later they meet in New York, where Nora now lives with her husband.

That's essentially it. And it's everything.

## Why It Works

Greta Lee and Teo Yoo are phenomenal together. The film's emotional power comes from what is *not* said — the looks held a beat too long, the pauses before changing the subject.

Song's screenplay understands that grief isn't only for the dead. You can mourn paths not taken, versions of yourself that dissolved when choices were made.

## Verdict

*Past Lives* is a small film in the best possible sense. It will stay with you long after the credits roll.
