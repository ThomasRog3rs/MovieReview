This movie has a masterful use of story hills, with problem after problem for Paddington and the humans to overcome. Some are personal challenges, some are challenges they must face together.

As kind hearted and cute Paddington is, I simply can’t trust anyone who actually enjoys marmalade. Marmalade is bitter, disgusting and rank! Anyone who likes such a rancid thing must be an absolute turncoat. This fascination on marmalade really distracted me from the story. I wonder what the writer intended with the use of marmalade? Was it to dehumanise the bear?!

Another thing that brought me out of the immersion of the cinematic experience was the lack of shock people had that a bear could talk!! Even Johnathan said “I didn’t know bears could talk” so it’s not like it’s a common thing in the cinematic universe.  So why weren’t these people not dumbfounded?! It really vexed me!

On the flip side, there was a short 3 second scene where the human family were speeding down a dual carriage way. As they swerved into another lane for an over take, a car behind them inexplicably swerved to one lane and then back again. At first this detail baffled me! I soon realised the driver must have assumed it was going to be an insurance scam where the family’s car would quickly break causing the behind car to crash into the back of them and be responsible for paying for damages. This extreme attention to detail expands the excellent world building. So the initial swerve was to avoid crashing into the back of them, once the driver realised it wasn’t the insurance scam, they committed to the second swerve to get back into the correct lane.
