Christopher Nolan's *Oppenheimer* is a three-hour fever dream about the man who split the atom and then had to live with what that meant. It is dense, demanding, and completely riveting.

## What Works

Cillian Murphy has never been better. His Oppenheimer is brilliant, vain, romantic, cowardly, and ultimately tragic — a man who understood physics far better than he understood power.

The Trinity test sequence is staged with the terrifying patience of a great thriller. Nolan withholds the sound for several agonising seconds, and when it arrives, the effect is overwhelming.

The non-linear structure — switching between Oppenheimer's 1950s security hearings and the wartime Manhattan Project — can be disorienting, but it mirrors the subjective, fractured nature of memory and guilt.

## The Caveats

At 180 minutes, some of the courtroom sequences outstay their welcome. Emily Blunt's Kitty Oppenheimer is given tantalising hints of complexity that the script never fully develops.

## Verdict

*Oppenheimer* is exactly the kind of ambitious, idea-driven blockbuster that Hollywood rarely attempts. A genuine event film.
