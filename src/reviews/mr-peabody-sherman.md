An incredible cinema piece about growth, realising and owning up to your mistakes, resilience, collaboration and adventure. A truly under rated movie, the absence of AI (Artificial Intelligence) and the focus on DI (Doggy Intelligence) is really refreshing in an animation. 

Mr. Peabody is clearly a high functioning and highly intelligent talking dog. He’s achieved amazing heights across disciplines from mastering multiple musical genres, a genius engineer and a polyglot. However, despite these vast achievements Mr. Peabody often exposes himself as a pathological liar making claims that are easily provably incorrect such as claiming he invented the famous Skateboarding move the “Ollie”. It is well known that Alan Gelfand invented this legendary move. Tho brings Mr. Peabody’s character into question, it could suggest deep rooted insecurities of needing to prove one’s self as the best person in the room… or could this highlight something more sinister like psychopathy?

For me, the true protagonist of the story is Sherman, who constantly shows his empathy, his eager for education and growth and his bravery facing danger. All of these qualities are underlined when he fights for improved equal rights for talking dogs, making a stance against speciesism.
