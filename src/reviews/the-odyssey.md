Despite some of the negative rhetoric you might see online, this movie is not a flop. Christopher Nolan has made yet another masterpiece showing off his excellence across multiple genres.

Nolan's use of up close and personal shots throughout the movie brought a sense of intimacy between audience and the story. This intimacy was made possible by the masterful world class performances from all of the cast. Not a single character felt flat or lacked depth, regardless of their total screen time. In particular, Matt Damon's performance was other worldly and likely will be seen as one of his greatest performances in the years to come. His emotional depth and range as an actor is incredible to watch on the big screen, his performance flawlessly covered rage, grief, anguish, regret and pride all without skipping a beat. But it wasn't just the scenes of emotional depth that were breathtaking, his high octane action sequences felt impactful and nostalgic of the *Jason Bourne* era.

Like many of his movies, Nolan shows off his impressive ability to tell complex stories in a non-linear fashion. He manages to seamlessly hop around time and locations without the flow being messy or confusing. Often with non-linear story telling I find writers can over complicate things, muddy the story telling and leave loose ends open making stories feel unfinished or unsatisfying. Nolan's real skill is being able to leverage the non-linear style to decide when he wants to let the audience in on some context from the past or future while maintaining a coherent storyline. It is truly impressive.

I've heard nobody else mention this so I might be completely on my own with this opinion. At the start of the movie I immediately felt like the voice acting was almost what you would expect from an on stage performance. As the movie led into the second quarter, this completely faded away with everyone giving stellar convincing performances. It is a minor point but one I wanted to share.

This is obviously another 5/5 film from Nolan which you should 100% make time to watch, in the cinema if possible. Do you really need to see it in IMAX "as Nolan intended" tho? I wouldn't say so! In my opinion, IMAX is overhyped. The technology behind it is cool, it gives you better visuals and more of them but without it there is nothing in the excellent story you miss out on. I didn't bother spending the extra money to watch this in IMAX, when I've seen IMAX movies in the past I find myself physically moving my neck just to watch two characters have conversations and I often come out with a headache for some reason... not exactly the elevated cinema experience its hyped up to be.
