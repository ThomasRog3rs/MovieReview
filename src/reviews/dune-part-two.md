Denis Villeneuve's *Dune: Part Two* is the rare sequel that surpasses its predecessor in almost every way. Where *Part One* was a measured, world-building exercise, *Part Two* is a full-throttle descent into myth, war, and fanaticism.

## The Good

The spectacle is unmatched. The Harkonnen arena sequence — shot in stark black-and-white — is one of the most viscerally exciting action scenes I've seen in years. Zendaya finally has room to breathe as Chani, and she makes the most of it, grounding the film's more operatic moments with genuine emotion.

Timothée Chalamet's Paul Atreides walks a genuinely uncomfortable arc. By the film's end, you're not sure whether to cheer or worry — and that ambiguity is the whole point.

> "He who can destroy a thing, controls a thing."

Hans Zimmer's score is once again extraordinary, weaving throat-singing and industrial percussion into something that feels alien and ancient at once.

## The Not-So-Good

The film's second act has a few pacing stumbles as it juggles too many subplots. The Emperor (Christopher Walken) feels underused, and a couple of supporting characters disappear without explanation.

## Verdict

Despite minor structural issues, *Dune: Part Two* is an extraordinary piece of science-fiction cinema — confident, visually stunning, and willing to let its hero be morally complicated. Essential viewing.
