Avatar sets the foundation of what became a Hollywood sci-if franchise, covering complex and emotional themes of humanity, compassion, capitalism, invasion, race and community.

It completely flips the script on the typical alien sci-fi by making humans the alien invaders. This approach forces viewers to think introspectively about what humanity means in a modern and post modern world, questioning our own morality as a species and to identify what motivates the blind oppression over what we have no rightful claim over. The story can be linked to atrocities throughout our own history, specifically imperialism and the expansion of the British Empire. 

The story is ambitious with its themes, and surprisingly the writers executed the vision excellently. The ability to invoke emotional responses from viewers by juxtaposing human compassion with human cruelty is something we don’t often see in mainstream Hollywood entertainment. This theme of compassion vs cruelty was a constant throughout the movie.

I did feel the abrasive nature of the military leader to be quite reductive and almost a trope of “mean military man just wants destruction”, I would have liked to see more depth into this character’s specific motivations rather than simply presenting him as an angry irrational speciesist. Along with this, I found the company’s leader’s switch from cold hearted profiteer to being filled with shame and moral guilt too sudden. The movie offered little explanation to the shift in his perspective, which left his character missing interesting insights that would have built on the thematic questions that were layered throughout.

This is a fantastic introduction to a cinematic universe and I hope the following movies can continue to impress. But for me, I can’t give this movie more than 3.5 stars because they used the fucking papyrus font for the logo!
